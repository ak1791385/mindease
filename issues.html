Common Mental Health Issues

Understanding the mental health challenges that many students face and how to recognize them.

Mental health challenges are more common than you might think. Recognizing the signs and understanding these issues is the first step toward seeking help and improving your well-being.

Anxiety

What is it?

Anxiety is a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease about something with an uncertain outcome. For students, it often relates to academic performance, social situations, or future concerns.

Signs & Symptoms
  • Persistent worry or fear
  • Racing heart or sweating
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Avoiding certain situations
  • Sleep disturbances
How to Deal with It
  • Practice deep breathing exercises
  • Break large tasks into smaller steps
  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule
  • Talk to trusted friends or family
  • Consider professional counseling

Depression

What is it?

Depression is a mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities that were once enjoyable.

Signs & Symptoms
  • Persistent sadness or emptiness
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Fatigue or low energy
  • Feelings of worthlessness
How to Deal with It
  • Maintain social connections
  • Engage in regular physical activity
  • Practice mindfulness and meditation
  • Seek professional help
  • Consider therapy or counseling

Exam Stress

What is it?

Exam stress is the feeling of pressure and anxiety that occurs before, during, or after examinations. It's a common experience that can significantly impact academic performance and well-being.

Signs & Symptoms
  • Difficulty sleeping before exams
  • Loss of appetite or overeating
  • Racing thoughts or mind blanks
  • Physical symptoms (headaches, nausea)
  • Procrastination or avoidance
How to Deal with It
  • Create a realistic study schedule
  • Use active study techniques
  • Take regular breaks during study
  • Practice relaxation techniques
  • Get adequate sleep and nutrition

Academic Burnout

What is it?

Academic burnout is a state of physical, emotional, and mental exhaustion caused by prolonged exposure to academic stress. It often results from overwhelming workloads and pressure.

Signs & Symptoms
  • Chronic exhaustion and fatigue
  • Cynicism about studies
  • Decreased academic performance
  • Feeling overwhelmed or detached
  • Physical symptoms and illness
How to Deal with It
  • Set realistic goals and expectations
  • Practice work-life balance
  • Take regular breaks and vacations
  • Engage in hobbies and interests
  • Seek support from counselors

When to Seek Professional Help

It's important to recognize when self-help isn't enough and professional support is needed.

Persistent Symptoms

Symptoms lasting more than two weeks without improvement

Impact on Relationships

Difficulty maintaining relationships with family and friends

Academic Decline

Significant drop in academic performance or attendance

Crisis Thoughts

Thoughts of self-harm or feeling like you can't cope

Remember

Seeking professional help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Mental health professionals are trained to help you develop effective coping strategies and work through challenges.
